## Template: Classic B2B Software

This template is designed to communicate trust, reliability, and professionalism to a business buyer. The visual language borrows from financial services — structured, serious, and precise — while remaining contemporary enough to signal a modern software product. Nothing here should feel like a consumer app or a creative agency site.

---

### Colors

**Background system:**
The page is built on three background values only. Pure white is the default. A very light cool gray — barely distinguishable from white — is used for alternating sections to create rhythm without visual noise. A deep, dark navy blue is reserved exclusively for two high-impact sections: the metrics strip and the final CTA band. No other background colors appear anywhere on the page.

**Brand color:**
The primary brand color is that same deep navy blue. It is used on the logo, the navigation links on hover, and as the color of section eyebrow labels. It should read as authoritative — the kind of blue you see in financial institution logos, not tech startup logos.

**Accent color:**
One vivid accent color is used for all primary CTA buttons, active states, icons, and highlighted elements. It must create strong contrast against both navy and white. Choose a saturated warm tone — a bold amber-orange or a clear electric blue — whichever feels more distinct from the navy. This accent appears sparingly. Its job is to make the primary button unmissable.

**Text:**
Near-black (not pure black — slightly warm or slightly cool) for all primary body text and headlines. Medium gray for secondary text, captions, and labels. Light gray for placeholder text and divider lines.

**Avoid:**
No gradients anywhere on the page. No purple, teal, green, or pink anywhere in the palette. No colorful illustrations or decorative color blocks.

---

### Typography

**Two fonts only, both from Google Fonts.**

**Display font (headlines and section titles):**
Choose a geometric, high-contrast display font — one that would look at home on a financial report cover or a serious editorial publication. It should feel strong and structured, not playful. It will be used at large sizes for the hero headline, section headlines, and the metrics numbers. Suggestions to match this brief: DM Serif Display, Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, or Fraunces. Do not use Inter, Roboto, or any neutral sans-serif for this role.

**Body font (all other text):**
Choose a highly legible, neutral sans-serif with excellent readability at small sizes — open letterforms, generous spacing, clear distinction between characters. It will be used for all body copy, navigation links, button labels, captions, and UI elements. Suggestions: DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, Outfit, or IBM Plex Sans. Avoid fonts that are too narrow, too stylized, or too closely associated with developer tooling.

**Type hierarchy rules:**
The hero headline is the largest text on the page. Section headlines are large but clearly smaller. Body text is comfortable to read — not too small, not too loose. Eyebrow labels above section headlines are always in the brand navy, in the body font, in all caps with wide letter-spacing. Metric numbers use the display font and are the boldest visual element on their section.

---

### Layout

**Container:**
All content lives in a centered column with generous horizontal padding. Nothing extends edge-to-edge except background color bands. The content column never feels cramped on desktop, and collapses gracefully to single-column on mobile.

**Section rhythm:**
Every section occupies its own full-width horizontal band with a distinct background (white or light gray, alternating). Sections are generously padded top and bottom — enough that each feels complete and breathable, not stacked on top of each other. The two dark navy sections (metrics and final CTA) break the pattern deliberately and should feel like punctuation marks in the page's flow.

**Grid:**
Feature cards use a 3-column grid. Product deep-dive rows use a 2-column layout alternating left/right. Testimonials use a 3-column grid. Pricing uses a 3-column grid. Nothing uses a 5-column layout or asymmetric arrangements.

---

### UI Details

**Buttons:**
Slightly rounded corners — not pill-shaped, not sharp. Enough radius to feel modern without feeling soft. The primary button uses the accent color as a solid fill. The secondary button is an outline/ghost — same color as the primary but with a transparent background and a visible border.

**Cards:**
White background. A single thin, light gray border. A very soft, diffuse drop shadow — barely visible, only present to lift the card off the page. Slightly rounded corners matching the buttons. No colored card backgrounds.

**Icons:**
Line-style icons only — consistent stroke weight throughout the page. All icons are a single color: either the brand navy or the accent color depending on context. Never multicolor, never filled/solid style. Use a single icon library (Lucide or Feather recommended) for visual consistency.

**Dividers and borders:**
Hairline weight — the thinnest visible line. Always in light gray. Used sparingly to separate footer columns and card edges. Never decorative.

**Shadows:**
Soft and diffuse only. Used on cards (very subtle), the sticky navigation bar when scrolled (a faint drop shadow to separate it from the page), and the product mockup frame. Never dramatic, never dark.

**No decorative elements:**
No abstract blobs, no gradient meshes, no background patterns, no illustrations, no stock photography. The only "imagery" on the page is the product dashboard mockup built in HTML and CSS.

---

### Navigation Bar

Sticky — fixed to the top of the viewport. White background. Gains a faint shadow when the user scrolls past the hero. Logo on the left. Five navigation links in the center. Two buttons on the right — a ghost "Log in" and a filled accent-color primary CTA button. On mobile, the center links collapse into a hamburger icon.

---

### Product Mockup

The hero must contain a product dashboard mockup built in HTML and CSS — not a placeholder box, not an image tag. It should show a realistic-looking software interface: a dark sidebar on the left with navigation icons and labels, a top bar with a search input and a user avatar, and a main content area showing at minimum two data visualization widgets (a line chart and a summary stat card) and a data table or list. Use the brand navy in the sidebar. Use the accent color for active states and chart highlights. This mockup communicates what the product looks like and is the most important visual element on the page.

---

### Page Sections (in order)

Build the page in exactly this sequence:

1. Sticky navigation bar
2. Hero — headline, subheadline, CTAs, trust line, product mockup
3. Social proof bar — "trusted by" + 6 placeholder logo slots
4. Features section — section headline + 6 feature cards in a 3-column grid
5. Product deep-dive — 3 alternating rows (text + UI panel mockup)
6. Testimonials — section headline + 3 cards in a 3-column grid
7. Metrics strip — 4 large numbers on dark navy background
8. Pricing section — 3 tiers, middle tier highlighted (use placeholder content)
9. Final CTA section — closing headline + buttons on dark navy background
10. Footer — 4-column link grid + bottom bar

---

### Quality Checklist

The output must meet all of the following:

- The page communicates the product's core value without any scrolling
- The hero dashboard mockup looks like real software — not a wireframe or a gray box
- Typography creates an unmistakable hierarchy: hero headline dominates, section headlines are clearly secondary, body text is clearly tertiary
- The two dark navy sections feel intentional and powerful — they should be the visual "anchors" of the page
- Every CTA button is immediately identifiable and stands out from surrounding content
- Hover states exist on all interactive elements — nav links, buttons, cards, and feature items
- The page is fully usable at 375px mobile width — no horizontal scroll, no overlapping elements
- All text passes contrast accessibility requirements against its background
- The sticky nav correctly picks up a shadow on scroll
- Code is clean and easy to edit — consistent class naming, no unnecessary inline styles
