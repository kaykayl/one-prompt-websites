# PART 2 — DESIGN SYSTEM

---

## Template: Modern SaaS

This template is designed for software products that want to feel inevitable — like the obvious, well-considered choice. The visual language is restrained and typographic. It communicates quality through what it leaves out: no heavy decoration, no aggressive color, no visual noise. The page should feel like the product itself is confident enough that it doesn't need to try hard. Think of the websites built by companies like Notion, Linear, or Vercel in their light mode — clean almost to the point of austerity, but with a warmth and precision that makes them feel human, not sterile.

---

### Colors

**Background system:**
The page is split into two distinct zones — the hero, and everything after it.

Everything after the hero lives on a clean off-white or white background. Sections are not differentiated by color — only by spacing. The one exception is the testimonials section, which uses a very light gray background for a single moment of visual separation. There are no dark sections anywhere on this page.

**Hero background — the one place color lives:**
The hero section has its own background treatment, separate from the rest of the page. It is not white. Build it as a very large, soft, radial gradient that bleeds across the entire hero area — starting from roughly behind the headline and fading outward toward the edges. The gradient uses two or three colors that are close to each other in hue — think of the kind of soft atmospheric color you see in the sky just after sunset. Muted purples bleeding into warm pinks, or soft blues into pale greens, or amber into rose. The colors should be highly desaturated and very light — almost pastel, but more sophisticated than pastel. They should feel like color is being whispered, not spoken. The gradient should never look like a CSS rainbow or a neon effect — it should look like light falling on a surface. The headline and subheadline text sit on top of this gradient and must remain fully readable in near-black. The gradient fades cleanly to white as it transitions into the section below the hero — there is no hard edge.

**Primary color:**
Pure black, or a near-black with the faintest warm undertone, is the dominant color across the entire page. It is used for the logo, all headlines, all body text, and the primary CTA button. Black is treated here not as harsh but as precise — the same way a well-printed book uses black.

**Accent color:**
Pick one color from the hero gradient and use it as a sparse accent across the rest of the page — appearing only in the product mockup's active states, the middle pricing card's border highlight, and the CTA button on hover. It should be slightly more saturated than it appears in the hero gradient, but still restrained. Its presence below the hero should feel like a deliberate echo, not a repetition.

**Text:**
Near-black for all headlines and primary body copy. A medium warm gray for secondary text, captions, and nav links in their default state. A lighter gray for dividers and borders.

**Avoid:**
No dark section backgrounds. No fully saturated bright colors anywhere on the page. No gradients outside the hero section. No color used decoratively below the fold — only for functional UI states.

---

### Typography

**Two fonts only, both from Google Fonts.**

**Primary font (used for everything):**
This template uses a single sans-serif font family for both headlines and body text — not two different fonts. Choose a geometric or humanist sans-serif that performs beautifully at both display sizes and small body sizes. It should have a large type family with enough weight variation to create hierarchy through weight alone, without needing a second typeface. The font should feel contemporary but not trendy — timeless enough that it won't date the site in two years. Suggestions: Geist, Instrument Sans, General Sans, or Bricolage Grotesque. Avoid anything that reads as technical or developer-coded — the goal is warmth and precision, not utility.

**How hierarchy is created:**
Since there is only one font, hierarchy comes entirely from size, weight, and spacing. The hero headline is large and heavy — the heaviest weight in the font family. Section headlines are medium-large and medium-weight. Body text is regular weight at a comfortable reading size. Labels, captions, and nav links are small and light. The contrast between a very heavy headline and very light body text on the same page is what creates the sense of visual sophistication in this style.

**Type details:**
Letter-spacing on the hero headline should be slightly tighter than default — pulled in just enough to feel deliberate. Line height on body text should be generous — more open than a typical document. All-caps is never used. No decorative text treatments.

---

### Layout

**Container:**
A narrower centered column than the Classic B2B template — content should feel like it has room to breathe. On desktop, the content column should occupy roughly two thirds of the viewport width, never stretching edge to edge. On mobile, full width with standard padding.

**Section rhythm:**
Sections are not separated by background color changes — they are separated by space. The vertical gap between sections should be generous enough that each section feels like its own chapter. There are no hard horizontal divider lines between sections. The page flows as one continuous surface.

**Hero layout:**
The hero is centered — headline, subheadline, and CTA buttons are all center-aligned on desktop. The section spans the full viewport width so the gradient background fills the entire screen. The text content sits within the narrower centered column. The product mockup appears below the CTA buttons, centered, and extends slightly wider than the text column — as if the interface is emerging from the page rather than sitting on it. The mockup sits at the bottom edge of the hero; the gradient fades to white just as the mockup comes into view, so the transition into the next section feels seamless. This is distinct from the Classic B2B two-column hero.

**Feature section layout:**
Features are not presented as cards with borders. Instead, they are arranged in a clean grid — icon above, title below, description below that — with generous spacing between items. The items define their own space through whitespace alone, with no border or background to contain them.

**Product deep-dive layout:**
Same alternating two-column structure as Classic B2B, but the UI panel mockups are displayed without a surrounding card or frame — they sit directly on the page background, with only a faint shadow suggesting depth.

---

### UI Details

**Buttons:**
The primary button is filled black — not navy, not colored, just black — with white text. It has a very slight border radius, barely perceptible. On hover, it shifts to the accent color. The secondary button is text-only with a thin underline on hover — no border, no background. This restraint is intentional: in this design language, a button that doesn't shout is more trustworthy than one that does.

**Cards:**
Used only for testimonials. White background, very faint border in the lightest possible gray, no shadow. Simple and flat. Feature items have no card treatment at all.

**Icons:**
Small, line-style, single weight. Used only in the features grid — one per feature, above the title. They are decorative anchors, not functional. Color matches the body text — dark gray, not accent color. Never larger than needed to orient the eye.

**Dividers:**
A single hairline border is used only in the footer, to separate the link grid from the legal bar below. Nowhere else on the page.

**Shadows:**
The product mockup in the hero has a single soft, large-radius shadow — diffuse enough that it reads as ambient light rather than a drop shadow. This is the only shadow on the page. Nothing else is elevated.

**No decorative elements:**
No background patterns, no grain, no blobs, no illustration. The product mockup is the only visual element that isn't text. The page's visual interest comes entirely from typography and spacing.

---

### Navigation Bar

Not sticky on this template — the nav scrolls away with the page. This is an intentional Modern SaaS convention: the page trusts the content enough not to chase the user. The nav is minimal: logo on the left, three to four text links in the center with no dropdown menus, and a single filled black primary CTA button on the right. No ghost "Log in" button — just one link labeled "Log in" in plain text. The nav has no background color or border — it sits directly on the page's off-white background.

**Exception:** On mobile, the nav becomes sticky and gains a white background with a faint border-bottom to stay readable.

---

### Product Mockup

The hero mockup is the most important element on the page and must be built entirely in HTML and CSS. It should look like a real, polished software interface — the kind you would see in an actual product demo. Show a clean top navigation bar, a left sidebar with icon-and-label navigation items, and a main content area with a mix of data elements: at least one chart (line or bar), one stat summary row, and one data table with real-looking placeholder rows. The interface uses a white background with very light gray borders between panels. The sidebar uses a very slightly darker off-white background — just enough to distinguish it from the main area. No dark mode in the mockup. The overall impression should be: this is a real product, and it looks good to work in.

---

### Page Sections (in order)

Build the page in exactly this sequence:

1. Navigation bar (not sticky on desktop)
2. Hero — centered headline, subheadline, CTAs, trust line, full-width product mockup below
3. Social proof bar — short centered text + 6 placeholder logo slots in a single row
4. Features section — section headline + 6 feature items in a 3-column grid, no card borders
5. Product deep-dive — 3 alternating rows (text + frameless UI panel)
6. Testimonials — section headline + 3 cards on light gray background
7. Metrics strip — 4 large numbers, but on the off-white background (no dark band — this distinguishes it from Classic B2B)
8. Pricing section — 3 tiers, middle tier highlighted with a black border (use placeholder content)
9. Final CTA section — large centered headline + buttons, off-white background, no dark band
10. Footer — 4-column link grid + bottom legal bar

---

### Quality Checklist

The output must meet all of the following:

- The hero gradient is soft and atmospheric — it should look like light, not a design asset
- The hero is the only section with color — everything below it is white, off-white, or light gray
- The gradient fades cleanly into white — there is no visible line where the hero ends
- The hero headline is the undisputed focal point — large, heavy, and sitting comfortably on the gradient
- The product mockup looks like a real, working piece of software that someone would enjoy using
- Hierarchy is achieved through typography weight and size alone — not color, not cards, not borders
- The accent color echoes the hero gradient but appears no more than three times below the fold
- There are no dark background sections anywhere — the page stays light from top to bottom
- The navigation disappears on scroll and does not follow the user down the page on desktop
- Feature items have no borders or card backgrounds — whitespace alone defines them
- Hover states are subtle — a slight darkening or underline, never a dramatic color shift
- The page is fully usable at 375px mobile width
- All text passes contrast accessibility requirements against the off-white background
- Code is clean and easy to edit — consistent class naming, no unnecessary inline styles
